Review: Orphan: First Kill
August 30, 2022

Orphan: First Kill
Horror/Thriller | 2022 | 1h 36min
Director: William Brent Bell

About the movie

Leena looks like a child but is actually 31 years old. That is because of a condition called hypopituitarism, which prevents her body from aging. She is a patient in an Estonian psychiatric clinic. One day Leena manages to lure a guard into her cell, and after murdering him, she steals his security card to escape. After the escape, she takes on the identity of a 4-year-long missing child from Amerika called Esther. Since she looks quite like what Esther would look like now, and considering the parents haven’t seen their daughter in 4 years, they take her in and don’t realize that it isn’t their daughter who is living in their house. Once a police officer checks if Esther’s fingerprints from 4 years ago match those from now because he grew suspicion about Leena, he is shocked to find out that they don’t match at all. Leena is forced to get rid of the police officer, who could blow her cover. Little did she know that her “Mother” knew about her lies, too, and she finds herself threatened by her. But since the mother didn’t want to hurt her husband with yet another disappearance, they worked out an agreement. Leena would go on to pretend to be Esther, and therefore the mother wouldn’t send her back to the psychiatric clinic. But Leena was not the only one who had to hide something…

What I think

If you are looking for a Psych thriller that will captivate you until the very end, then “Orphan: First Kill” is definitely a great choice. The movie was the opposite of boring; in fact, it was so tense and exciting that I wouldn’t even pause it for a bathroom-break. The plot was amazing. The plot twist even better. The tension never eased at any point, and was accompanied on many occasions by scares and creeps.

At the beginning of the movie, I was rather alarmed by Leena’s behavior, but as the story developed and the surprising plot twist came along, the mother showed to be even more creepy. In the end, I really felt bad for Ellen, who had no bad feelings but ultimately lost everything.

What’s more to mention is the amazing acting. Isabelle Fuhrman, as Leena resp. Esther was amazing and played the stone-cold character very well.

Plot: 10/10
Originality: 8/10
Cinematogrophy: 6/10
Acting: 8/10
Entertainment: 9/10
Rewatchability: 7/10
Overall: 8/10
