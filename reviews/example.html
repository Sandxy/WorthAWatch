About the movie

After NAME1 car broke down in the middle of nowhere, she happens to fall into the hands of a psychotic and coldblooded killer. After her chopped up body was found in the river, her fiancé NAME2 dedicates his life to the mission of making the killers life miserable.

But he made one big mistake: he underestimated the killer and soon enough, the game gets turned around

What I think

I sat through those 2.28 minutes at the edge of my seat. The story grabbed me immediately and didn’t let go until the last minute. Sure, the plot isn’t that original, as we all have already seen probably around a dozen movies where the fiancé goes after the girlfriend’s killer. However, this movie did not get boring at any point throughout the whole time. It get’s spiced up by some real disturbing scenes, like a cannibal enjoying his human-flesh-dinner.

One thing that definitely surprised me, was the killer’s willpower to live, because that man had to take in and live through lot of hits. The director did not hesitate to involve some disturbing scenes, or characters, looking at you Tae-joo.

What’s to say about the acting performance? Well, it was amazing. Not only Choi Min-sik’s performance was, just like in “Oldboy”, remarkable, but also the main character’s, Lee Byung-hun, was awesome.

(I always have to complain at least about one thing: I personally would not consider “I saw the devil” as a horror movie, but more like a thriller/action movie)
