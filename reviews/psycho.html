Review: Psycho
August 17, 2022

Psycho
Horror/Thriller | 1960 | 1h 49min
Director: Alfred Hitchcock

About the movie

After Marion stole 40k from her employee, she leaves town. On her journey she stops at a motel to get some rest. Little did she know that this place would be the last one she’d ever see in her live...

What I think

There is no doubt that Alfred Hitchcock did amazing work with this movie, but I personally have to say that it was not for me. Some scenes where undoubtedly tense but it just wasn’t enough for me to get sucked into it. I also wasn’t blown away by the reveal that his mother didn’t really exist, or rather existed as a part of him, because I saw it coming – which might have been a lucky guess, but it still takes away the surprise factor which might have led to me enjoying the movie a bit more. It’s a rather slow-paced movie with a few jump scares and also some kind of “creeping me out” scenes, but it was just not enough to keep me expectant in front of the tv, nor did it give me that feeling of “I absolutely need to know what’s going to happen next”.

Altogether I’m glad I finally got around to watch such a classy movie, even though I didn’t like it as much as I would have hoped to.

Plot: 7/10
Originality: 7/10
Cinematogrophy: 10/10
Acting: 8/10
Entertainment: 7/10
Rewatchability: 3/10
Overall: 7/10
