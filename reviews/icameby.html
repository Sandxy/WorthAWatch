Review: I Came By
September 6, 2022

I Came By
Thriller | 2022 | 1h 50min
Director: Babak Anvari

About the movie

Toby originally just wanted to break into Hector's house to spray some graffiti on his walls, but when he was inside the house he found something very disturbing. Shocked, he leaves and tries to get his friend's help, who refuses. So Toby has to go alone. A week after his horrifying discovery, he breaks in again and attempts to free the prisoner he found a week ago in Hector's basement. To his misfortune, Hector comes home earlier than planned and surprises Toby with a cricket bat smashing against his skull.

Toby’s mother calls the police after she didn’t hear from her son for three days and they begin to investigate his disappearance. Although Hector is a suspect in Toby’s missing case, the police didn’t find any evidence to support these suspicions. The mother takes it on herself to find out what happened to her son and breaks into Hector's house. Just like her son, she was surprised by him and she faces the same ending as her son.

What I think

Normally the main character in a movie never dies, or at least not until the very end, but this movie completely ignored that “rule”. I liked that, because it added another layer of suspense and surprise to it, being that literally anyone could die. But not only did that make the movie intense and interesting to watch, the unpredictable choices and decisions of the characters did that as well. In the end though, I wasn’t really blown away by the movie; however, it wasn’t bad either, so overall it was okay.

Plot: 7/10
Originality: 6/10
Cinematogrophy: 6/10
Acting: 6/10
Entertainment: 6/10
Rewatchability: 5/10
Overall: 6/10
