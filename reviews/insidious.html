Review: Insidious
August 17, 2022

Insidious
Horror/Thriller | 2010 | 1h 43min
Director: James Wan

About the movie

A family of 5 moves into a new house. But not long after they’ve moved in, some weird things start happening. Footsteps on the attic, doors open on their own and the worst thing, after a fall off a ladder, the little boy Dalton falls into some sort of coma. It’s all too much to handle for them, so they decide to move out of the haunted house. But the nightmare doesn’t seem to stop in the new house either…

What I think

This was one tense movie! I watched it a very long time before and I definitely didn’t remember it to be so good. I was definitely holding my breath for a few scenes and gasped at some other ones. Though the idea of strange things happening in a house is unquestionably not a new one, this movie somehow made it not seem boring to me. The whole thing where Josh had to leave his body in his sleep and go find his son was a bit weird and also the “farther-world” wasn’t how I imagined it to be.

But, oh boy, did I not see that ending coming. The moment I realized it, I was too stunned to speak. This movie ending will undoubtedly be remembered by me as one of the best horror movie endings I have ever seen.

It got me excited for the second movie, so stay tuned for that review!

Plot: 7/10
Originality: 5/10
Cinematogrophy: 8/10
Acting: 9/10
Entertainment: 10/10
Rewatchability: 10/10
Overall: 8,2/10
