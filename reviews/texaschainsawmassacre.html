Review: Texas Chainsaw Massacre
August 18, 2022

Texas Chainsaw Massacre
Horror/Thriller | 1974 | 1h 23min
Director: Tobe Hooper

About the movie

Two siblings and three of their friends are on a road trip to visit the sibling’s uncle’s grave. When they visit the old house that belonged to their uncle, they fall into the hands of a crazy cannibal family.

What I think

WOW. I don’t think I have ever watched a movie where a character screamed that much. I mean, I get it. I would scream too, if a crazy dude with a mask, and a chain saw would chase me, but THIS much? No way that actress had a voice after those takes for the next three months. Anyway, the movie was alright. Some scenes were rather bloody and aggressive, but for me it wasn’t as scary as I imagined it to be. Sure, maybe back in ’74 it probably was one of the scarier movies, but nowadays I don’t think it is that scary anymore; at least that’s how I feel.

Plot: 7/10
Originality: 7/10
Cinematogrophy: 4/10
Acting: 3/10
Entertainment: 5/10
Rewatchability: 2/10
Overall: 4,7/10
