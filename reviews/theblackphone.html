Review: The Black Phone
August 23, 2022

The Black Phone
Horror/Thriller | 2022 | 1h 42min
Director: Scott Derrickson

About the movie

In the town where Finney lives, all of a sudden, children start to disappear. For the third kidnapping Finney gets taken when he walked home after school. He gets locked up in a basement, which has nothing more in it but a mattress, a toilet, and a broken black phone, which doesn’t work anymore because its wires have been cut. Throughout the time Finney has to spend in the room, he receives some phone calls over the broken phone by the other boys who have been kidnapped, who try to help him out. Because they failed to kill the kidnapper, who hides his face under a mask, they want Finney to kill him. Meanwhile, his sister is getting some weird dreams, which she can’t believe are just dreams. She is convinced that they are real and that they would somehow lead to the place all the missing children have been taken to.

What I think

I really liked this movie. It brings a lot of tension and disturbing scenes with it, which I really enjoyed. No part got me bored or felt dragged out. I really can’t say anything bad about this movie! What’s even more amazing is that the acting was amazing as well. I also found the plot to be very exciting and tense, in addition to not being predictable as well.

Plot: 9/10
Originality: 8/10
Cinematogrophy: 7/10
Acting: 9/10
Entertainment: 10/10
Rewatchability: 9/10
Overall: 8,7/10
