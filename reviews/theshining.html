Review: The Shining
August 18, 2022

The Shining
Horror/Mystery | 1980 | 2h 26min
Director: Stanley Kubrick

About the movie

5 months in a big luxurious hotel together doesn’t sound to bad, right? That’s why Jack took the job to look after the hotel during the winter and moved there with his wife and their son. But life in complete isolation doesn’t seem to be affecting John’s sanity too well, and shortly after they’ve moved in, John loses his mind.

What I think

What obviously must be pointed out is the amazing acting performance of Jack Nicholson. He did an awesome job playing the role of Jack Torrance and it was just entertaining to watch it. The cinematography in general was amazing as well and it just gave the movie the right feeling. The movie was rather slow-paced, which slowly built up the insanity in Jack and also the tension for the movie itself. However, it was too slow-paced for my taste, which led to me being a bit bored throughout the movie.

Plot: 7,5/10
Originality: 8/10
Cinematogrophy: 9/10
Acting: 10/10
Entertainment: 7/10
Rewatchability: 4/10
Overall: 7,6/10
