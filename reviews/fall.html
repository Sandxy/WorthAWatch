Review: Fall
September 13, 2022

Fall
Thriller | 2022 | 1h 47min
Director: Scott Mann

About the movie

After the death of her boyfriend Dan, due to a rock-climbing-accident, Beckys life was shattered. One year after her big loss, her best friend Hunter comes to town again. To take Becky’s mind off of things a little bit, Hunter wants to go on one of their adrenaline adventures, they always went to, before Dan’s tragic death. For this adventure, Hunter chose the 2000 feet tall B67TV tower. Together they drive out in the middle of nowhere, where the tower is located, and climb to the top of it. Once they take their pictures and videos, they want to go down again, but as Becky steps on the ladder, the whole thing collapses, leaving them trapped 2000 feet from the ground. Hopelessly they are waiting for help, when in the evening they spot two men in an old rv, who, with the help of a signal pistol, notice the two girls on top of the tower. But instead of helping them, they steal their car and drive away, leaving them with less hope than ever. 50 ft underneath the top of the tower, where the two girls are trapped, is their backpack which got caught there. Hunter climbs down the rope she’d brought with her, to get the backpack. When climbing back up again, though, Hunter can’t make it and she falls down.

Becky is now left alone on the platform. After she fails to send a message via the drone Hunter has brought with her, she almost gives up. Grasping at the last straw, her last opportunity to get help, she throws her phone down to the ground to send a message to her father, hoping it would be sent and he would rescue her.

What I think

I was afraid that the movie would get really boring, since the majority of it is just showing the two girls on their tiny platform on the top of the tower. But luckily, my expectations were proven wrong. The whole movie was very intense and it never got boring. Just be aware to watch this, if you are afraid of heights, because even though I am not, my stomach turned around a few times regardless. Great movie that kept me entertained and quite stressed out as well.

Plot: 8/10
Originality: 5/10
Cinematogrophy: 7/10
Acting: 8/10
Entertainment: 8/10
Rewatchability: 6/10
Overall: 7/10
