Review: Sinister
August 17, 2022

Sinister
Horror/Thriller | 2012 | 1h 50min
Director: Scott Derrickson

About the movie

An author moves with his family into a new house because the story he wants to write about, took place there. He wants to write about the family that got hang there, and other, perhaps connected, murders as well. But, clearly, living in a house where a whole family got murdered can never be a good idea, as he, soon enough, had to find out himself.

What I think

I had somewhat high expectations for this movie, so I was strongly disappointed when it didn’t meet them. Don’t get me wrong, the whole thing about the murders and how they were connected was great and all, but it just wasn’t tense or thrilling enough for me. I can’t say anything bad about the acting either because it was really great. The movie also wasn’t predictable, because I did not see the ending coming, but overall it just didn’t offer me the excitement I would expect for such a highly rated horror movie.

Plot: 6/10
Originality: 6/10
Cinematogrophy: 8/10
Acting: 8/10
Entertainment: 7/10
Rewatchability: 7/10
Overall: 7/10
