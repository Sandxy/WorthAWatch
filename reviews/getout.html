Review: Get Out
August 18, 2022

Get Out
Horror/Thriller | 2017 | 1h 44min
Director: Jordan Peele

About the movie

Chris gets invited to the house of his girlfriend’s parents to meet them for the first time. He being an African American, he had his concerns about the reaction of his girlfriend’s parents, but the parents seemed very nice. At least they did at first. Not long after that, Chris must experience horrors he couldn’t have even imagined.

What I think

At first, I was already prepared to be disappointed as I had the hypnotized thing already seen coming and thought that was going to be the whole story, but it certainly was not. I was actually quite shocked when the “Bingo” – scene came on. I really liked the whole plot, and it wasn’t a boring movie at all. It had some tense moments and, even if only for the last few minutes, some bloody scenes as well. (Pretty satisfying ones though, because him getting his revenge was quite cool, to be honest) The movie itself wasn’t too tense or terrifying, but the story sure was. That, compared with some great acting performances made the movie quite great and enjoyable.

Plot: 9/10
Originality: 8/10
Cinematogrophy: 6/10
Acting: 8/10
Entertainment: 9/10
Rewatchability: 7/10
Overall: 7,8/10
